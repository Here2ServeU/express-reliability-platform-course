# Interview and Client Playbook

Use this as your standard narrative when presenting the capstone.

## 1) 90-Second Platform Introduction

"I built a reliability platform that progresses from local service delivery to cloud-native regulated operations. The platform includes secure identity federation, infrastructure as code, observability, SLO-driven incident response, AIOps-assisted triage, and cyber-physical simulation workflows. It is designed for healthcare and fintech environments where reliability, auditability, and controlled change are mandatory."

## 2) 15-Minute Interview Walkthrough

1. Problem framing (regulated reliability pain points)
2. Architecture overview (services + control plane)
3. Security and identity approach
4. SRE and incident operations model
5. AIOps and simulation results
6. Lessons learned and measurable outcomes

## 3) Client Discovery Questions

- What regulatory obligations are in scope?
- What are your top three business-critical services?
- What are your current outage patterns and mean time to recovery?
- What evidence is required for audits?
- What release governance and segregation of duties are required?

## 4) Client Delivery Blueprint

- Week 1–2: baseline architecture and controls
- Week 3–4: observability + SLO alignment
- Week 5–6: incident runbooks and simulations
- Week 7+: optimization, automation, and handoff

## 5) Evidence You Should Always Show

- Architecture diagram and environment boundaries
- SLO/SLI catalog and dashboards
- Incident runbook execution sample
- Terraform plan/apply traceability
- Access control and CI/CD federation proof

## 6) Differentiators for Students

When interviewing or advising clients, emphasize:

- You can build and operate, not just deploy
- You use objective reliability targets (SLOs), not opinions
- You can connect engineering implementation to compliance and audit evidence
- You can explain complex systems clearly to leadership and auditors
